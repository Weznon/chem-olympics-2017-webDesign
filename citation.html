    • ProCon.org bills itself as “the leading source for pros and cons of controversial issues”. It is a highly reliable site, as it has a team of researchers dedicated to educating the public about important and controversial issues by providing unbiased, nonpartisan information. ProCon has received a platinum rating for transparency from GuideStarb, the primary resource for assessing non-profits.
    • Procon Org. Rep. GuideStar, 2007. Web. 02 Apr. 2017 Note: GuideStar has a database about all IRS-registered 501(c) organizations, of which there are approximately 850,000. GuideStar receives a lot of its information from the IRS, but it also receives much of its information from the organizations themselves - this means that the information about Procon is in part decided by ProCon. However, GuideStar is the most thorough database for non-profits in existence today, and one would be hard pressed to find a better source.
  • Huntington, Scott. “The History of Performance-Enhancing Drugs.” Sports Then and Now , Sports Then and Now Website Group, 18 Apr. 2014, sportsthenandnow.com/2014/04/18/the-history-of-performance-enhancing-drugs/. Accessed 2 Apr. 2017.
    • Sports Then And Now operates a national website and a boston area website. Both are focused on providing news, history, and other information to american sports fans. The article is a few years old, but it is not outdated beyond reason, though it is brief and focuses on the implications of the history on sports today.
