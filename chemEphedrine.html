  1. Formation

 

Ephedrine is created via a derivative of phenylalanine. By being exposed to the enzyme ammonia lyase cinnamoyl-CoA can be created.  The cinnamoyl-CoA is then reacted with a hydratase to attach the alcohol functional group. The product is then reacted with a retro-aldolase, forming benzaldehyde. Benzaldehyde reacts with pyruvic acid to attach two carbon units. This product then undergoes methylation and transamination to form ephedrin

  1. Structure-C10H15NO

  2. Effects-As a central nervous system stimulant, ephedrine increases basal metabolic rate (BMR), meaning the body burns calories faster. It is also able to induce fat loss via increasing the amount of fat available for fuel as well as by increasing heat expenditure. It has been implicated in increasing the metabolic rate by up to 5% in humans. Ephedrine also interacts with muscle cells, increasing heat expenditure in them as well as fat cells. It can also prevent the breakdown of muscle tissue to a small degree.
  3. Detection

Ephedrine can be detected via urine analysis using techniques such as liquid chromatography and gas spectroscopy.
