  1. Formation:HGH is created through the expression of the growth hormone genes 1 and 2 in the human genome. The more recent method of artificially synthesizing this hormone is via e.coli with the genes from humans via plasmid transformation.
  2. Structure: C225H360N76O67S

  3. Effects- Somatropin (HGH) functions by sending a signal to the cells in our muscles, bones and fat tissue. This signal promotes muscle growth and affects fat loss. The functions of the hormone also increase gluconeogenesis and promote insulin resistance thereby providing reduced sensitivity to insulin. All of this results in an increase rate of fat loss.
  4. Detection The human liver completely metabolizes the hormone to its component amino acids thus the human growth hormone has very small traces in urine if any, making the drug extremely hard to trace.
