Corticosteroids

  • https://cn.agilent.com/cs/library/applications/5989-3395EN.pdf

 

 

  • Formation - Corticosteroids are produced as a derivative of cholesterol. The adrenal gland can modify cholesterol to produce corticosteroids. Any artificial production of this steroid models the adrenal gland’s behavior.
  • Structure
  • Effects Corticosteroids come in two varieties. The main variety used as performance enhancing drugs are glucocorticoids, which reduce inflammation. The other variety, mineralocorticoids, help retention of salt and are rarely used in doping contexts
  • Detection Use of mass spectrometry can isolate corticosteroids, along with a number of other performance enhancing drugs, in urine. Use of this technique was successful in the 2004 olympics.
