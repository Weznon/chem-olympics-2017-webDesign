  • Formation
  • Corticosteroids, a derivative of Cholesterol, are produced by the adrenal gland, which does so by modifying Cholesterol. Artificial productions of Corticosteroids mimic the processes of the Adrenal gland
  • Structure

  • Effects
  • Corticosteroids are formed in two types: glucocorticoids, which reduce inflammation and mineralocorticoids, help retention of salt. Glucocorticoids are the more commonly used, in doping contexts, of the two
  • Detection
  • The technique of mass spectrometry can be used to identify corticosteroids, along with a number of other performance enhancing drugs, in urine. This technique was used in the 2004 Olympic Games, resulting in the disqualification of several athletes
