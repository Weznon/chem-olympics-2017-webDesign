Testosterone

  1. Formation
    1. Testosterone is derived from cholesterol, similarly to other steroids.

 

  1. Structure

  1. Effects
    1. Testosterone helps build muscle in users by binding to an androgen receptor. Once it has bonded it formulates a signal transduction pathway that leads to the expression of proteins needed to build muscle, resulting in faster regrowth of damaged tissues.
  2. Detection
    1. Testosterone can be detected in urine using liquid chromatography and gas spectroscopy, methods used to discover other steroids. However, this technique does not discriminate between normal and artificial testosterone. To avoid this pitfall, the amount can also be tested, to see if it falls within normal ranges
