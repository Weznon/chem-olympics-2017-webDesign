  1. Formation
    1. Salbutamol is a modified acetophenone molecule that is derived from salicylic acid. Salicylic acid can be produced by the lysis of aspirin.
  2. Structure

    1. C13H21NO3    1-(4-hydroxy-3-hydroxymethyl phenyl)-2-(t-butylamino)-ethanol sulphate

 

  1. Effects
    1. The butyl group in Salbutamol increases the chemical’s ability to bind with beta 2 receptors. Beta 2 receptors’ signal transduction pathway cause muscles to relax, leading to the lungs gaining more air. For athletes to get more air in every breath is advantageous as performance (namely speed) can improve without athletes having shortness of breath. The injection of Salbutamol also can lead to increases in muscle buildup, another desirable trait for athletes, who might want strength along with their super lungs.
  2. Detection
    1. Salbutamol can be detected in urine using liquid chromatography and gas spectroscopy, followed by inspection for beta agonist residue. The disadvantage to this method is that the procedure and equipment are expensive. This means that only organizations and people with a lot of money can use this detection method, and that organizations that can't afford these machines cannot disprove salbutamol use.
