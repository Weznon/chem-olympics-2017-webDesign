  1. Formation- Salbutamol is a modified molecule from acetophenone which is derived from salicylic acid. Salicylic acid can be produced through the lysis of aspirin.
  2. Structure: C13H21NO3    1-(4-hydroxy-3-hydroxymethyl phenyl)-2-(t-butylamino)-ethanol sulphate
  3. Effects- The butyl group in Salbutamol increases its affinity for beta 2 receptors. Beta 2 receptors via a signal transduction pathway cause muscles to relax leading to the lungs gaining more air. For athletes to get more air in every breath, is highly favorable as performance can go up without athletes having shortness of breath. The injection of Salbutamol also can lead to increases in muscle buildup.
  4. Detection - Salbutamol can be detected in urine using liquid chromatography and gas spectroscopy for beta agonist residue. The disadvantage to this method is that the procedure and equipment are expensive. This means that only organizations and people with a lot of money could use this detection, and that organizations that can't afford these machines cannot disprove salbutamol use.
